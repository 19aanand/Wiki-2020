Silver


  • Engineering Success
    • Although we do not have a wet lab component due to the pandemic, we have outlined transformation and HiFi protocols that we would have followed if we did have access to a lab. We hope that these protocols provide useful information for what we hope to accommplish with our project.These can be found on our Engineering Success page
  • Collaborations
    • We have created a an educational video with Ohio State University highlighting the benefits of biological killswitches and addressing common myths associated with GMOs. Through this, we hope to educate the community about the benefits that can come about through genetic engineering.
    • We created a poster with Oneonta to display around campus to teach our school community about GMOs. Our poster's constitute facts about GMOs, etc.
    • We pursued a collaboration with MSP and others to peer-review each other's papers. This is extremely helpful because, through this, we were able to learn from each other's projects and improve our skills in terms of communicating our work to the public
    • We have also participated in many social media collaborations! For more information please visit our Collaborations page
  • Human Practices
    • We have met with many farmers and professors that have provided us with unique insights. Their advice allowed us to improve our approach and design so it is best suited for the environment when implemented. We also learned that many people are hesitant to support GMOs due to the fact that there is a lack of transparency between the scientific community and the general public. We also learned that farmers do not use GMOs because they are scary, but rather because their consumers prefer eating organic products. Because of this, our team has decided to put a large focus on establishing education initiatives to lessen the divide between scientists and the general public, which can be found in the Education page. For more information on the farmers and professors we contacted, please visit our Human Practices page
  • Proposed Implementation
    • We hope to implement our project into vertical farms and greenhouses. With the level of control provided by our system, farmers that tend to a wide variety of crops can grow genetically modified crops without worrying about them encroaching on their wild type counterparts. Feel free to visit the Implementation page to learn more
