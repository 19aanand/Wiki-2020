Attributions



Stony Brook iGEM would like to thank the following groups/individuals for their support and expertise. Without their help, our project might've never come to fruition




J. Peter Gergen

Dr. Gergen, as the faculty advisor of all Stony Brook iGEM teams, has been crucial to our success and the team's development throughout the year. Whether imparting knowledge on laboratory procedures or aiding us in contacting experts in our field of research, he's been there every step of the way. In many ways, he's the face of Stony Brook iGEM, and the best advisor we could ask for.





















Steven E. Glynn

Dr. Glynn helped us understand the biological processes around programmed cell death and apoptotic mechanisms in cells. His advice was crucial for us to understand how to model protein-protein interactions with software, and refining our construct to work as intended.





















Gabor Balazsi

Dr. Balazsi played a huge role in understanding what and how to model with our project this year, as well as advising us on what our plasmid should include to benefit the next phase of our team. His knowledge on fluorescence and cell growth helped us focus on when our construct will be expressed in terms of cell differentiation.
